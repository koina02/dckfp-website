2025 Diplomatic Program Schedule

Comprehensive training and engagement programs advancing Kenya's foreign policy objectives

About Our Annual Diplomatic Program

The Diplomatic College of Kenyan Foreign Policy's 2025 program represents our most ambitious initiative to date, designed to equip Kenyan diplomats and international relations professionals with the skills, knowledge, and networks needed to advance Kenya's interests on the global stage.

This year's program emphasizes practical diplomacy skills, regional cooperation, and multilateral engagement, with special focus areas including climate diplomacy, digital governance, economic partnerships, and peacebuilding.

Regional Focus

Deep engagement with East African Community, African Union, and other regional bodies

Expert Faculty

Learn from seasoned diplomats, academics, and policy experts with real-world experience

Practical Training

Simulations, negotiation exercises, and field visits to diplomatic missions

Networking

Connect with fellow diplomats, government officials, and international partners

Monthly Program Breakdown

Explore our detailed monthly schedule below. Each month focuses on specific thematic areas while building cumulative knowledge and skills.

January Schedule

  • January 10-12, 2025
    Orientation & Welcome Ceremony

    Official opening of the 2025 program with keynote address by the Principal Secretary for Foreign Affairs. Overview of program objectives and expectations.

    Main Auditorium, DCKFP Campus
    Featured Speakers: Dr. Amina Mohamed, Prof. Patrick Lumumba
  • January 15-19, 2025
    Foundations of Kenyan Foreign Policy

    Comprehensive review of Kenya's foreign policy evolution, constitutional framework, and current strategic priorities.

    Seminar Room A
    Instructors: Amb. Monica Juma, Dr. Macharia Munene
  • January 22-26, 2025
    Diplomatic Protocol & Statecraft

    Practical training in diplomatic protocol, negotiation techniques, and crisis management simulations.

    Diplomacy Lab
    Instructor: Amb. Kiplagat

February Schedule

  • February 3-7, 2025
    East African Community Policy Workshop

    Deep dive into EAC structures, policies, and Kenya's role in regional integration. Includes simulation of EAC Council of Ministers meeting.

    EAC Hall
    Facilitator: Dr. Richard Sezibera
  • February 10-14, 2025
    Trade Negotiations Simulation

    Practical exercise in regional trade negotiations, covering tariff policies, non-tariff barriers, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

    Negotiation Lab
  • February 18-21, 2025
    Field Study: Border Communities

    Field visit to Kenya-Uganda border to study cross-border trade dynamics and community relations.

    Busia Border

Registration Information

The 2025 program is open to Kenyan diplomats, foreign service officers, and international relations professionals. Limited spaces are available for qualified applicants from partner institutions.
