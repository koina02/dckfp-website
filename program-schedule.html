2025 Program Schedule

Our comprehensive annual program designed to advance Kenya's diplomatic engagement

About Our Annual Program

The Diplomatic College of Kenyan Foreign Policy's annual program is carefully structured to provide comprehensive training, research opportunities, and policy engagement activities that align with Kenya's strategic foreign policy objectives. Our 2025 program builds on our successful initiatives while introducing new elements to address emerging global challenges.

This year's program emphasizes practical diplomacy skills, regional cooperation, and multilateral engagement, with special focus areas including climate diplomacy, digital governance, and economic partnerships.

Core Focus Areas

Regional diplomacy, multilateral engagement, economic partnerships, and emerging global challenges

Program Duration

January - December 2025, with intensive modules and ongoing research projects

Target Participants

Diplomats, foreign service officers, policy analysts, and international relations professionals

Certification

Participants receive certification upon completion of each module and the full program

Monthly Program Breakdown

Explore our detailed monthly schedule below. Each month focuses on specific thematic areas while building cumulative knowledge and skills.

January Schedule

  • January 10-12, 2025
    Orientation & Welcome Ceremony

    Official opening of the 2025 program with keynote address by the Principal Secretary for Foreign Affairs. Overview of program objectives and expectations.

    Main Auditorium, DCKFP Campus
    Featured Speakers: Dr. Amina Mohamed, Prof. Patrick Lumumba
  • January 15-19, 2025
    Foundations of Kenyan Foreign Policy

    Comprehensive review of Kenya's foreign policy evolution, constitutional framework, and current strategic priorities.

    Seminar Room A
    Instructors: Amb. Monica Juma, Dr. Macharia Munene
  • January 22-26, 2025
    Diplomatic Protocol & Statecraft

    Practical training in diplomatic protocol, negotiation techniques, and crisis management simulations.

    Diplomacy Lab

February Schedule

  • February 3-7, 2025
    East African Community Policy Workshop

    Deep dive into EAC structures, policies, and Kenya's role in regional integration. Includes simulation of EAC Council of Ministers meeting.

    EAC Hall
  • February 10-14, 2025
    Trade Negotiations Simulation

    Practical exercise in regional trade negotiations, covering tariff policies, non-tariff barriers, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

    Negotiation Lab
  • February 18-21, 2025
    Field Study: Border Communities

    Field visit to Kenya-Uganda border to study cross-border trade dynamics and community relations.

    Busia Border
  • February 24-28, 2025
    Regional Peacebuilding Seminar

    Examining Kenya's role in regional conflict resolution with case studies from South Sudan and Somalia peace processes.

    Seminar Room B

March Schedule

  • March 3-7, 2025
    UN System & Kenya's Engagement

    Comprehensive overview of UN structures and Kenya's strategic priorities within the multilateral system.

    UN Simulation Room
  • March 10-14, 2025
    International Law Intensive

    Focus on treaty law, diplomatic immunity, and maritime boundary disputes with practical case studies.

    Legal Studies Center
  • March 17-21, 2025
    Model UN Security Council

    Simulation of UNSC proceedings focusing on current global security challenges.

    Diplomacy Lab
  • March 24-28, 2025
    AU Legal Frameworks Workshop

    Examination of African Union legal instruments and their implementation in Kenya.

    AU Hall

April Schedule

  • April 7-11, 2025
    Trade Policy & Negotiations

    Analysis of Kenya's trade policy framework and practical negotiation skills development.

    Trade Policy Center
  • April 14-18, 2025
    Investment Promotion Strategies

    Case studies of successful investment attraction campaigns and Kenya's comparative advantages.

    Seminar Room C
  • April 21-25, 2025
    Field Study: Export Processing Zones

    Visit to Athi River EPZ to study practical aspects of trade and investment facilitation.

    Athi River EPZ

May Schedule

  • May 5-9, 2025
    Global Climate Governance

    Analysis of UNFCCC processes and Kenya's leadership in climate negotiations.

    Environmental Studies Center
  • May 12-16, 2025
    Green Economy Strategies

    Exploring opportunities in renewable energy, carbon markets, and sustainable infrastructure.

    Innovation Hub
  • May 19-23, 2025
    Field Study: Climate Projects

    Visit to geothermal and wind power projects to study Kenya's renewable energy diplomacy.

    Olkaria & Lake Turkana

June Schedule

  • June 2-6, 2025
    Digital Governance & Cyber Diplomacy

    Examining Kenya's digital foreign policy and engagement in global internet governance forums.

    Digital Lab
  • June 9-13, 2025
    Emerging Technologies Workshop

    Exploring diplomatic implications of AI, blockchain, and other disruptive technologies.

    Tech Innovation Center
  • June 16-20, 2025
    Social Media for Diplomacy

    Practical training in digital communication strategies for diplomatic engagement.

    Media Studio

Registration Information

The 2025 program is open to Kenyan diplomats, foreign service officers, and international relations professionals. Limited spaces are available for qualified applicants from partner institutions.
